Singapore Airlines Limited
Annual Report FY2022/23 - Selected Financial Highlights and Management Discussion
(Financial year ended 31 March 2023)

FINANCIAL PERFORMANCE

Singapore Airlines (SIA) Group reported record operating profit of SGD 2.69 billion for the financial year 2022/23, a turnaround from the operating loss of SGD 0.48 billion in FY2021/22. Net profit attributable to equity holders reached SGD 2.16 billion, the highest in the airline's 76-year history.

Group revenue rose 93% to SGD 17.78 billion as travel demand rebounded strongly post-pandemic. Passenger revenue more than doubled to SGD 13.69 billion. Cargo revenue declined 29% to SGD 3.19 billion as the air freight market normalised following the pandemic-driven surge.

PASSENGER OPERATIONS

Passenger flown revenue grew 112% as capacity expansion met surging leisure and business travel demand. Singapore Airlines carried 35.0 million passengers in FY2022/23, compared to 8.7 million in FY2021/22. Passenger load factor improved to 85.6%, approaching pre-pandemic levels of approximately 88%.

Revenue per available seat-kilometre (RASK) was 11.0 cents, while cost per available seat-kilometre (CASK) was 8.8 cents, yielding a positive unit margin for the first time since FY2018/19.

The carrier restored 100% of its pre-pandemic network of over 130 destinations by the end of FY2022/23. Capacity, measured in available seat-kilometres (ASK), reached 87% of pre-pandemic levels. The group added 7 new destinations and resumed services to 18 destinations suspended during the pandemic.

Singapore Airlines launched services to new destinations including Medan (Indonesia) and Chiang Mai (Thailand) to capture growing regional travel demand. Codeshare agreements with 32 partner airlines provided connectivity to over 1,000 destinations worldwide.

SCOOT SUBSIDIARY

Scoot, SIA's budget carrier, reported its first annual profit since its founding in 2011. Scoot carried 12.0 million passengers at an average load factor of 87.3%. The low-cost carrier operated 73 aircraft to 66 destinations.

Scoot expanded its Airbus A320 family fleet to capture short and medium-haul leisure travellers in Southeast Asia. The airline's ancillary revenue per passenger grew 14% as customers purchased bundled packages including seat selection, meals, and checked baggage.

CARGO OPERATIONS

SiaFreight revenue fell 29% to SGD 3.19 billion as belly cargo capacity increased with passenger flight restoration, reducing demand for dedicated freighter capacity. Average yield per freight tonne-kilometre declined 43% from the exceptional levels seen during the pandemic.

Despite lower yields, cargo profitability remained positive. The SIA Cargo fleet of 7 freighter aircraft was optimised for high-value, time-sensitive cargo including pharmaceuticals, semiconductors, and fresh produce. Revenue freight tonne-kilometres declined 17% to 7.7 billion.

FLEET AND CAPITAL EXPENDITURE

SIA Group operated 207 aircraft as at 31 March 2023, consisting of 147 passenger aircraft for Singapore Airlines and 60 aircraft for Scoot. The group accepted delivery of 9 aircraft during the year, including Airbus A350-900s and Boeing 737-8s.

Capital expenditure was SGD 3.4 billion, primarily for aircraft acquisition and cabin retrofitting. SIA announced orders for 20 additional Airbus A350-900 aircraft and 20 Boeing 737-8 aircraft to support fleet renewal and capacity growth through 2030.

The average age of the Singapore Airlines passenger fleet was 7.0 years, one of the youngest in the industry. Young aircraft consume 15-20% less fuel per seat compared to older generation aircraft, providing a structural cost advantage.

FUEL AND SUSTAINABILITY

Fuel costs represented 23.8% of total expenditure. SIA consumed 5.89 million tonnes of jet fuel in FY2022/23. The airline hedged approximately 30% of its projected fuel requirements for FY2023/24 using a combination of forward contracts and options.

SIA committed to achieving net-zero carbon emissions by 2050. The airline increased its use of sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) to 1.0% of total fuel consumption in FY2022/23, a tenfold increase from FY2021/22. SIA partnered with five SAF producers to secure supply through 2030.

The group's carbon intensity, measured in grams of CO2 per revenue tonne-kilometre, declined 4.2% year-on-year due to improved load factors and younger fleet efficiency.

BALANCE SHEET

Total assets were SGD 44.5 billion as at 31 March 2023. Shareholders' equity stood at SGD 14.8 billion, recovering to pre-pandemic levels. Net cash position (cash and short-term investments minus borrowings) was SGD 3.2 billion, the strongest financial position since 2015.

The company repaid SGD 2.5 billion in mandatory convertible bonds issued during the pandemic. Gearing ratio (net debt to equity) improved from 0.62 in FY2021/22 to a net cash position.

DIVIDENDS AND SHAREHOLDER RETURNS

The Board proposed a final dividend of SGD 0.38 per share for FY2022/23, the first dividend payment since FY2018/19. Total dividends for the year were SGD 0.38 per share, representing a payout of approximately 28% of full year net profit.

The Board also announced a share buyback programme of up to SGD 500 million over 18 months, reflecting confidence in the group's financial position and growth prospects.

OUTLOOK

Management expected passenger demand growth to moderate from the pandemic recovery levels but remain positive. Corporate travel was recovering more slowly than leisure travel, with approximately 80% of pre-pandemic corporate booking volumes recovered.

The airline guided for FY2023/24 capacity to reach approximately 95% of pre-pandemic levels by the end of the financial year. Competition was expected to intensify as Asian carriers accelerated capacity restoration, particularly on North Asia routes.

SIA was expanding in higher-margin long-haul markets including the United States, Europe, and Australia. The launch of non-stop Singapore-New York services in October 2023 was expected to capture premium business travel demand.

CUSTOMER EXPERIENCE

Singapore Airlines maintained its position as one of the world's top-rated airlines in independent passenger surveys. The airline invested SGD 1.1 billion in cabin upgrades, introducing new Business Class suites on Boeing 777-300ER aircraft.

KrisFlyer, SIA's loyalty programme, had 3.2 million active members. Members redeemed 12.4 billion KrisFlyer miles in FY2022/23, including significant redemptions for hotel stays and credit card rewards as travel resumed.

The airline's customer satisfaction score for in-flight service improved to 4.7 out of 5.0, driven by crew training programmes and improved service delivery processes post-pandemic.
